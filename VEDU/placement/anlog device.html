Analog Devices

Analog Devices, Inc., also known as ADI or Analog, is an American multinational semiconductor company specializing in data conversion and signal processing technology, headquartered in Norwood, Massachusetts

The company manufactures analog, mixed-signal and digital signal processing (DSP) integrated circuits (ICs) used in electronic equipment.These technologies are used to convert, condition and process real-world phenomena, such as light, sound, temperature, motion, and pressure into electrical signals.

Headquarters: Norwood, Massachusetts, United States

CEO: Vincent Roche

Founded: 1965, Cambridge, Massachusetts, United States

Subsidiaries: Linear Technology, Analog Devices Gen. Trias Inc, more

Founders: Ray Stata, Matthew Lorber

Products Semiconductors

official Website :http://www.analog.com/en/index.html

Selection Process:

The company conducts recruitment process every year to select new candidates. The selection process of the company consists of 3 rounds. These rounds are as follow

  • Aptitude Test
  • Group Discussion
  • HR + Technical Interview

Academic Criteria:

  • A total of 60 percent throughout (Class X, XII and B.Tech).
  • No backlogs.
  • Education gap of up to 1 year.

Pattern of Written Exam:

Section Time allotted (in mins)
Aptitude 30
Technical 100
Total 130

Quantitative Aptitude
  • opamp circuits
  • stability feedback amplifers
  • analysis of circuits with capacitors, resistors and inductors.
  • transistor models
  • implementations with universal gates
  • counters
  • determining unknown digital circuit blocks(similar to blackbox)

Interview Experience


I submitted my resume during a Career Fair, where I was asked some digital design questions on-spot.

I got a on campus interview call the very next day. On campus interview was 1 hour long. Mostly questions related to Floating Point Binary Arithmetic, Setup hold time, Verilog, State machines etc.

Following this I got 2 telephonic interviews. None of them were scheduled. Questions asked basically related to projects, internship, DFT algorithms, design…

I found most of the questions pretty simple. I am categorizing all the questions below for reference.

1. Latches - Flip Flops
  1. Basic operation
  2. Setup and hold time - especially for latch
  3. Meta stability
  4. Code in verilog
  5. How to avoid unintentional latches
  6. Problems with SR latch and how are they solved
2. FIFO
  1. Constraints on reading and writing pointer
  2. Size of fifo for given input and output frequencies
  3. FIFO for data and address buses
  4. How to synchronize?
3. Design
  1. Asynchronous and synchronous reset in Flip Flops
  2. Data(D1) following data(D) while going low (asynchronous) but synchronous with clock while going high
  3. ADC Design
  4. Sensitivity list - if missed what is implication on gate level simulation
  5. Clock by three
4. DFT
  1. Explain DFT
  2. Scan Chains and Scan FFs
  3. Fault models
  4. Delay fault
  5. Testing of two clock counters by adding logic in between to reduce overall time. (First counts from 0 to 255. For every 256 counts in first seconds increments its counter by 1.)
5. Verification
  1. Test plan
  2. Assertions
  3. Coverage - when will you stop verification
  4. Layered test bench
  5. Randomization
  6. Difference between rand and randc.
  7. where is randc used? - decoder verification
  8. Classes and objects
6. Computer Architecture
  1. Explain stages of pipeline
  2. Cache design
  3. Associativity of cache
7. ASIC
  1. Explain ASIC flow
  2. Explain DRC and LVS
  3. Explain synthesis script

What is the package given by Analog Devices to UG engineering students?

A2A I was doing an internship there and I heard it is somewhere around 7-8 LPA if you are from IIT/BITS etc

How are the working hours?

Flexible. You might end up working over time to deliver on time, but company and managers are flexible to give you off. Very attentive to employees feedback and they count on annual reviews and will take actions to address concerns.

What questions did they ask during your interview?

Can you work on-call rotation with a small team? Are you willing to go to training and travel if needed. Both Systems and Network support are going to be part of your job are you prepared for that?

What is the hiring process of Analog devices in India?

The hiring for fresh graduates is largely done through on campus recruitment from premier institutes. a written examination is conducted first for students with CGPA above a certain limit. There are separate examinations for Analog and Digital profile. Depending on the number of vacancies to be filled, a few selected candidates will be called for interviews. Typically, there are two rounds of technical interviews and an HR interview.
