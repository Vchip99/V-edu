Syntel

Syntel is a global IT service provider. It also provides Knowledge Process Outsourcing solutions. The company was founded in 1980 and headquartered in Troy, Michigan. The company mainly functions in financial services, healthcare, insurance, retail & automotive verticals

CEO: Nitin Rakesh (21 Apr 2014)

Revenue: 966.6 million USD (2016)

Headquarters: Troy, Michigan, United States

Number of employees: 22,795 (March 31, 2017)

Founder: Bharat Desai

official Website : http://www.syntelinc.com/

Selection Process:

Syntel company conducts recruitment process every year to select new candidates. The selection process of the company consists of 4 rounds. These rounds are as follows:

  • Written Exam
  • Group Discussion
  • Technical Interview
  • HR Interview

Academic Criteria:

  • 55% throughout the academic career(Class X, XII, Graduation and post graduation).
  • Pending backlogs are not allowed.
  • Maximum gap of 1 year is allowed.

Interview Experience

About Syntel process, total four rounds which are,

1. Online Aptitude Test.
2. GD.
3. TI.
4. HR.

# Aptitude test:

Held on 15 January 2016. The test has three section:

1) Quant.
2) Verbal.
3) Logical.

It was quite easy than other aptitude tests but here you have to attempt 90 questions in 60 min. Be fast while solving apti questions, because not enough time. With each section has its own time. Just go through R. S. Aggarwal and IndiaBix. Each Section has 30 questions and each sections has time limit of 20 minutes. But as no negative marking is there I have given answers of all unsolved questions.(I think attemption of all question increases your chances to clear the apti). After 3-4 days I got a mail that, I am shortlisted & next rounds will be at Syntel in Talawade, Pune on 20 Jan. 2016.

# GD:

Group of 10 members was formed and given 2 min exactly for preparation on the topic. Actually at that time we all people in our group (7 people) didn't get the meaning of the topic. I got a very genuine panel. He has very frank and told us the meaning & then started a GD. Its something like Forcing someone is good or bad. I mentioned some real time examples related to our subject. He praised me for that :). 10-15 mins results are declared. Out of 7, 3 were selected. And I was one of them. (Just speak something in GD. Don't keep quiet)

# TI:

Tech person was good and very kind. He asked me introduce myself. I did that. Then he started questions. Those are as follows:

1. Logic for identifying Prime number & in JAVA. (Mentioned in my CV about Java that's why)

2. Difference between C, Cpp, Java.

3. How many joins in a SQL? Write a query for each of those joins?

4. Tell me about your final year project?

(Not confident for programs but did with confidence). Then he said that is enough. After 10 mins results are declared and I have cleared the tech round also.

You have any questions for me? I asked him what actual things you look in a candidate? He said, the basics concepts and whatever you have done till now is checked. Be confident in everything if you don't know the answer. How many joins in a SQL? Write a query for each of those joins? also. Give some related answer if you are blank. But don't show him that you don't know the answer. Confidence is very important. After 5 mins results are declared and I have cleared the tech round also.

# HR Round:

Groups are formed and we were 2 people in a group. HR Person is a lady. She was good and very kind.

She asked only one question,

You have any questions for me?

I asked her, what are the priority goals for company this year? &
About training location. That's it.

On the 2nd of Feb, in the evening, I got an email that was ecstasy for me. Golden words for any fresher - "You have been selected".
