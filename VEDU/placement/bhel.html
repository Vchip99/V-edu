BHEL

Bharat Heavy Electricals Limited owned by Government of India, is a power plant equipment manufacturer and operates as an engineering and manufacturing company based in New Delhi, India.


Industry Electrical equipments

Founded 1964

Founder Government of India

Headquarters New Delhi, India

Area served Worldwide

Key people Atul Sobti, (Chairman & MD)

Products
  • Gas and Steam Turbines
  • Boilers
  • Electric Motors
  • Generators
  • Heat Exchangers
  • Pumps
  • Switchgears
  • Sensors
  • Automation and Control Systems
  • Power electronics
  • Transmission systems etc.

official Website : http://www.bhel.com

Selection Process:

Company conduct recruitment process every year to select new candidates. It can also select candidates through GATE exam. The selection process of the company consists of 3 rounds. These rounds are as follows:

  • Written Exam
  • Technical Interview
  • HR Interview

Academic Criteria:

  • 60 percent or above in B.Tech , Class X and XII.
  • Gap of upto one year is allowed.

Interview Experience

after written test on aug 10th I had interview on sep 26th... results were out on 12th sep and I got selected. Coming to BHEL company's profile its an Navratna PSU having an profitable track record... So I am very happy to work for it..
Now coming to the written test paper
120 Technical (am from Electrical branch)
120 Aptitude + English
1/5 negative marks

Technical questions were quite tough. I did it for half an hour and I could do only 15 questions then I jumped to Aptitude section just to have a glance of it I found it very easy... So out of 120 I cracked around 80-95 questions. then  I again jumped to technical sections in this phase I could do around 35-45 questions and time limit of 150 mins was over.

Technical Paper
1) Simple questions from inter physics some around 10-15 questions like formula of radius of gyration, sphere, cylindrical
formula of K.E
2) Most of the questions were from Control systems very easy (nearly 20 questions)
3) Machinery questions were quite tough (10 questions nearly)
4) Power systems (20 questions)
5) Electrical measurements and instruments
What I suggest is to do control systems, power systems and machinery gate materials

Interview consists of choosing a chit which contains specific topic I got applications of synchronus machines. so if you answer this you could gain good marks... for interview you should be thorough with each and every topic of electrical bcause we had around 100 chits.. after that they asked basic questions.. it lasted for half and hour atlast I got selected for Heavy Power Equipment Plant (HPEP), Hyderabad.

How can I prepare for BHEL trainee engineer written or interview?

Even at BHEL they usually follow the same process , that is Aptitude , GD , technical round and HR. And aptitude is just to check your problem solving skills and the time you take to do it. GD is just to check your communication skills and the confidence in your voice.Please be confident while talking. Technical round is completely based on your specialization , that is Computer science , Mechanical , Electrical. According to me this the most difficult obstacle for any engineer to clear the interview and prove his talent, So i suggest you , make your basics strong and make sure you go through the company portal and have a complete look on their technologies . Spend about 5 to 6 hours for preparing on those technologies.

How does BHEL recruit engineers?

BHEL was recruiting engineers through All India Common entrance Test as well as through campus placement also till 2011. But in 2011, Supreme Court asked PSU not to recruit through campus placements. So they have started recruiting through GATE.

First of all, apply GATE exam in nov-dec, and then by using GATE application number apply for BHEL recuritment form through

careers.bhel.in.

Now clear GATE exam with >65 marks for General, >60 marks OBC and >55 marks for SC.

On the basis of your rank in GATE or GATE score, M/s BHEL (Also other PSU like NTPC, IOCL, HPCL, BARC etc.) announced their shortlisted candidate list.

BHEL has not been recruiting for the past 2 years.

BHEL's do business is that of power equipment manufacturing and installation. Due to the slow-down in power sector and infrastructure as a whole in from last two years, the company has been facing severe problem in the order book. Around 5-6 years ago, the sector looked upbeat and 300 odd ETs were getting inducted every year however, soon after coal scam broke out and new power plants stopped coming up. For this reason, it is no longer feasible for the organization to recruit.
